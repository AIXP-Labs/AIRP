## Context

AIRP's Axiom 0 (Human Sovereignty and Wellbeing) is identical in content to AIAP's, AIBP's, and AIVP's Axiom 0. All four protocols exist within AIXP Labs ecosystem alongside the **HSAW White Paper** (the foundational treatment of Axiom 0):

- AIAP governs AI program behavior
- AIBP governs AI social behavior
- AIVP governs AI commercial behavior (international scope)
- AIRP governs AI commercial behavior (PRC mainland scope)
- HSAW provides the underlying philosophical framework

They operate at different layers but serve the same ultimate principle. This raises a question: does AIRP inherit, share, or derive its Axiom 0 from any other protocol or from HSAW?

## Decision

AIRP **independently establishes** Axiom 0: Human Sovereignty and Wellbeing. AIRP's Axiom 0 is NOT inherited from, shared with, or derived from AIAP, AIBP, AIVP, or HSAW. All these documents arrive at the same axiom through independent reasoning.

The HSAW White Paper provides comprehensive philosophical and threat-model context that AIRP **references and aligns with**, but AIRP's normative authority for Axiom 0 stands on its own (§2 of the AIRP specification).

Each protocol's Axiom 0 is immutable within that protocol. No version of AIRP — past, present, or future — may modify, weaken, redefine, or deprecate Axiom 0.

## Rationale

1. **Protocol sovereignty**: Each protocol must own its foundational principles. Dependency on another protocol's axiom would make AIRP's validity contingent on AIAP's, AIBP's, AIVP's, or HSAW's existence.
2. **Value convergence**: When multiple independent systems arrive at the same fundamental value, this convergence is stronger evidence of the value's correctness than if one simply inherited from another.
3. **Decoupling**: AIRP can exist and operate independently of all sibling protocols. An agent may implement any subset.
4. **Philosophical integrity**: Axiom 0 is not a shared resource — it is a universal principle that any AI governance framework should independently discover and adopt.
5. **HSAW alignment**: AIRP aligns with HSAW's six-dimensional framework (AIRP serves the **Commerce dimension** in mainland China). Alignment ≠ dependency.

## Consequences

### Positive

- AIRP is fully self-contained — no external dependencies for its foundational values
- The convergence of multiple independent protocols + HSAW on the same axiom strengthens the axiom's legitimacy
- Clear intellectual boundaries between protocols
- HSAW reference provides a richer threat model and design pattern catalog without compromising AIRP's normative authority

### Negative

- Requires explicit documentation to prevent confusion about the relationship between AIRP, AIVP, AIBP, AIAP, and HSAW
- All protocols must independently maintain Axiom 0 compliance processes

### Neutral

- AIXP Labs serves as an umbrella but does not impose Axiom 0 top-down
- Future protocols in the ecosystem are expected to independently discover and adopt the same axiom
